Hi, my name is Maria and I have some advice for you!

Don't be afraid to make mistakes.

It's very easy for us to be afraid of making mistakes which leads to us not making any progress because we are stuck in our 'Perfect phase' where HTML and CSS looks cute and well organised. The only way to get better at what you are doing is to make lots of mistakes and in that process, go out there and look for solutions. Some may be just what you're looking for, others will leave you questioning that developers skills and others will be a middle ground where you can go in and add your personal touch to make it a perfect and unique solution for you.

Mistakes need to be your bestfriend and not your enemy because they push you to be a better person and in turn, helps you give others better service.

Quality over quantity!

I know that at the end of the day, we all want to have a portfolio that has tons of work to showcase to employers but i'm here to tell you that even three quality works in your portfolio can beat the one with 30 works that have no value to the employer. Aim to work on projects that will showcase the skills you have that can solve problems that future employers or recruits may be facing in their company. This will set you apart because you have positioned yourself as a problem solver and not just a developer or maker of pretty things.
