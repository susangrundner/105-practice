Practice, Practice, Practice!

  • When you start coding your first website, you are doing something that you have never done before. It's a new challenge, a new opportunity, and it's exciting! There are so many little details to remember, but the more you do it, the more information you retain and soon, you'll be fluent in a whole new language!
  • Don't be afraid to start again. I got caught in a rut a few times and completely deleted all of my HTML and CSS and started from scratch. The second go around often ended up completely error free!
  • Take advantage of all of your online resources. There are so many free projects out there to keep you coding and practicing all of your new skills.

Push Through the Hard Times

  • Confusion can set in, frustration can overcome us. Be patient with yourself but keep going! There is light on the other side!
  • This is a normal part of the process of learning a new technology, and in fact, it's a critical part of the process.
  • You see when you encounter a new programming language part of your learning process includes learning to train your eye. The way to train your eye is to read a good deal of code even when you don't totally understand what it's doing.
