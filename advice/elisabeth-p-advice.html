Hi, my name is Elisabeth and I have some advice for you!

Face your fears

Everything is new, I plan to make a living out of web-development - as a freelance in Tanzania, whereras my customers will probably be elsewhere, but probably not in Tanzania. Usually I am very sponatneous, I don't plan too much and things are working out. That's also how other peole see me and they are surprised when I tell them that I am anxoius sometimes. I often wonder how I will manage to make my dreamy come true. How will I be able to convince future clients that I am good to work with even thugh I am far away? Will I manage it or will I have to resign at one point and go back to Austria? Sometimes I feel such a pressure that I can't focus or feel like I have to do everything right away. I have learned to take a break, do some Yoga or go outside. I ask myself what is scaring me so much and after figuring it out I make friends with my fears, accept them but encourage myself to not let them put me down.

Coding can be fun but also distressing

Fun learning doesn't mean that it's always fun. It's like life. While doing the Jubilee Austin project, I felt like I have no clue what I am doing - it was kinda frustrating. So before taking the next step I decided to practice. It's like doing a riddle, it tastes your patience but once you have solved the problem, you feel soooo good - like a hero. But the next riddle will bring you down to earth again ;) But the more you practice the more fun it is and then it's also nice to join the Skillcrush community to help others and improve your skills.

Practice brings joy!

Have a vision

Planning in these uncertain times are difficult. But I think it is always good to have a vision. And there are many ways to come closer to your dreams. And who knows, maybe the way will alter your vision?!

The cool thing with coding is, that you sometimes think that something is super difficult, but after some days of practice the 'difficult thing' seems to be so easy.
