Hello. I'm Jared. There were many names on the list, but you clicked mine. I hope you're not disappointed.

My main piece of advice: Don't forget what brought you here!

If you're like me then you're likely learning to code as a way of making a positive change in your life. Maybe you want a new or better job, maybe you want to start making extra money on the side, or maybe you just want to learn a new creative outlet as a hobby. Whatever the case, you have a goal which is motivating you to learn how to code, and it's important to keep that goal in mind.

Just today, I debated whether to put in an hour or two of study or just take a break tonight. Then I remembered, the reason why I'm studying coding in the first place is so I'll have a more flexible schedule and can do work during the times that work best for me rather than on someone else's schedule. Remembering this caused a surge of inspiration to overcome me. I sat down, and I got to work!

My other piece of advice: Remember to believe in yourself!

You have to stay positive as you work! Learning a new skill to a professional level is tough, but that's what we've all set out to do. Everyone has to start from somewhere, and even the most advanced coder started with no knowledge -- the same as you and me. To get to that expert level takes a lot of work, but it's totally within your ability to do so. Encourage yourself and keep at it!
