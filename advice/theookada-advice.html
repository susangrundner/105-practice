Theo here! Here is a small bit of advice for you!

Hesitation is normal, but try it anyways!

I've struggle (and still do) with anxiety and thinking "I don't think I am smart / brave / fast enough" to pursue something that I am interested in. Programming and Coding are excellent examples of this. I have always wanted to try it, but I was afraid of not being good enough to try it. So I went and studied something that I convinced myself that I liked which was Hospitality Management.

Oh man, that was a mistake. Not only did I think I wasn't good enough for that industry, I felt I made a horrible mistake and was ashamed about it and got depressed.

I took some time and rethink my life and decided I should pursue what I want to do, which was more technology and web based. Now that I am with Skillcrush, I feel like I am smart enough and it is quite fun.

In life hesitation is normal, because you want to make the "right" decision. But trying is still important, because regardless of the result you will end up more mature than before.

Do it until it is correct

Mistakes are an opportunity to do better the next time. Just be humble when there is a mistake and focus on making it right.
