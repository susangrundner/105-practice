Hi, my name is Thomas and I have some advice for you!

It's a marathon, not a sprint!

Try and take things slowly at first. You want to be able to spend as much time as possible practising a certain skill before you move onto the next one.

For example, I have found learning Git very challenging, so I will be sepnding the next week or so going over the same concepts to make sure I have a good understanding of them before I move on.

Get involved with Slack!

The coding community is huge. Why let that easy-to-reach resource go to waste? Slack is great because it is full of students working on the same things as you are!

Make sure you ask somebody for help if you are ever stuck or unsure about something. We're all here to help and most of us love a good challange!

Don't forget your cheatsheets!

Nobody is expecting you to remeber all that code. Make sure you utilise the cheatsheets as much as possible - after all, it's not actually cheating!

Remembering certain bits of code will come with experience, time and practice. So what are you wating for? Get going!
