Hi, my name is Rebecka and I have some advice for you!

Be a Slacker!

Slack is a great tool for not only getting answers to your inevitable questions, but also for giving you the opportunity to help others in the Skillcrush community! I highly recommend not only using Slack when you have a problem, but also checking in each day or as often as your schedule permits.

Sharing successes as well as roadblocks, and seeing others encounter them as well, helps me to realize that we're all in this together. Despite doing this coursework 100% from my living room, I feel connected to the others around the world who are also engaged in learning about coding & design.

Keep on Truckin'

I had the idea early on in my Skillcrush career that I'd take a break after completing the HTML and CSS course to work on our family business website update. I thought that with another example of a website that I coded on my portfolio, this would be helpful for me to show potential clients.

After taking a few weeks off to work on that, I realized that if I kept working on the Skillcrush coursework as it's laid out, I would be better equipped to update our website once I was done with the Responsive Website course, and even Javascript.

If you're feeling like you should be doing something else as well as the Skillcrush coursework, I would encourage you to just stick to the Skillcrush curriculum and power through. I feel like once I get through Git, Responsive Websites, and Javascript, I'll be able to work much more efficiently on our family business website. I'm wrapping up Git soon, and we'll see how I feel after finishing the next few courses!
